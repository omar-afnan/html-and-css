This page is a tribute to Steve Jobs

Early Life and Education:

Steve Jobs was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, to Abdulfattah Jandali and Joanne Schieble.
He was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs, a couple from Mountain View, California.
Jobs grew up in Cupertino, California, and developed an early interest in electronics and technology.

Co-founding Apple:

In 1976, Steve Jobs, along with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne, co-founded Apple Computer, Inc. in the Jobs family garage. Their first product, the Apple I, was a single-board computer designed by Wozniak. The success of the Apple II, introduced in 1977, played a crucial role in Apple's early growth.

Departure from Apple:

In 1985, due to internal conflicts, Jobs left Apple after a power struggle with the company's board of directors. He went on to found NeXT, a computer company, and also acquired The Graphics Group, which later became Pixar Animation Studios.

Return to Apple:

Apple struggled during Jobs' absence, facing financial difficulties and a lack of innovation. In 1996, Apple acquired NeXT, and in 1997, Jobs returned to Apple as the interim CEO. His leadership marked a period of revitalization and innovation, leading to the introduction of iconic products like the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad.
